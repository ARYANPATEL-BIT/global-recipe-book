Chicken Kottu

A tasty mix of chopped godamba roti, chicken curry, vegetables, and egg, all cooked together. Quick, easy, and full of flavor - a Sri Lankan favorite enjoyed by everyone.

Prep20 minutes
Cook20 minutes
DifficultyEasy
Street Food Spicy Savory
Ingredients
  • 2 Godamba Roti pieces (or Parotta/Roti Canai)
  • 1 cup Chicken Curry (gravy only)
  • 4 pieces Chicken from curry or roast chicken
  • Â½ cup Carrot, cut into thin strips
  • Â½ cup Leek (green part), cut into thin slices
  • Â¼ cup Tomato, chopped (or 1 small tomato)
  • 3-inch piece Leek (white/light green), cut into rings
  • 2 Green Chilies, sliced (or chili flakes)
  • 1 tsp Garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp Ginger, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp Onion, chopped
  • 1 Egg
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • Salt, to taste
Tips & Variations
  • Use leftover chicken curry for authentic flavor and convenience.
  • Adjust the spice level by adding more or fewer green chilies.
  • For vegetarian version, substitute chicken with paneer or mixed vegetables.
  • Add cabbage or bell peppers for extra crunch and nutrition.
  • The key to great Kottu is the chopping technique - use two metal spatulas for authentic street-style preparation.
Method
  1. Heat the oil: Heat oil in a large pan or wok on medium-high heat. Add the chopped ginger, garlic, and the white part of the leek. Cook for about 30 seconds until it smells good.
  2. Add the chicken: Add the chicken pieces from your curry (or roast chicken). Break them into small pieces using your spatula. Cook for a minute or two until they start to turn slightly brown.
  3. Cook the egg: Push everything to one side of the pan. Crack the egg into the empty space and let it fry for a few seconds. Stir and break the egg into small pieces, then mix it with the chicken.
  4. Add onions and chilies: Move the mix to one side again and add the chopped onion and sliced chilies. You can also add some chili flakes for extra spice. Stir everything together well.
  5. Add vegetables: Move everything to the side again and add the carrot strips. Stir them for about 30 seconds to soften a little. Then add the chopped tomatoes and mix everything together.
  6. Season the mixture: Add salt to taste and mix well. You can also add a little pepper if you like. Taste and adjust the seasoning.
  7. Add roti and leeks: Add the chopped godamba roti pieces and the green part of the leeks. Mix everything well on high heat for about a minute.
  8. Add curry sauce: Pour in the chicken curry gravy. Add more if you want a soft, saucy Kottu or less if you like it dry. Mix until all the roti pieces are coated with the curry.
  9. Final mixing: Lower the heat and keep mixing and chopping the roti pieces using your spatula for 1â€“2 minutes. This helps blend all the flavors together.
  10. Serve: Serve hot straight from the pan with some extra curry or chili sauce on the side.
