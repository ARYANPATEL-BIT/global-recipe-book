Brigadeiro Recipe

A classic Brazilian chocolate truffle made with sweetened condensed milk, cocoa, and butter. Easy to prepare and perfect for celebrations.

Prep: 10 min
Cook: 10–12 min
Yields: 16–20 pieces

Ingredients

  • 1 can (395 g / 14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder (sifted)
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter (plus extra for rolling)
  • Chocolate sprinkles or grated chocolate (for coating)
  • Paper candy cups (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a medium, heavy-bottomed saucepan, combine the sweetened condensed milk, sifted cocoa powder, and butter.
  2. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon or heatproof spatula. Scrape the bottom and sides to prevent burning.
  3. After about 8–12 minutes, the mixture will thicken and start to pull away from the bottom of the pan — it should be glossy and dense. This is the right consistency for rolling.
  4. Remove the pan from heat and let the brigadeiro mixture cool to room temperature. For easier handling, refrigerate it for 30–60 minutes until firm enough to roll.
  5. Lightly butter your hands. Scoop small portions (about 1 teaspoon) and roll into smooth balls.
  6. Roll each ball in chocolate sprinkles, then place in paper candy cups. Keep refrigerated until serving.

Tips & Variations

  • For fudgier brigadeiros, cook slightly longer until mixture becomes thicker and darker.
  • Flavor variations: add a teaspoon of instant coffee, a splash of rum, or a pinch of sea salt to enhance the chocolate flavor.
  • Use cocoa powder for a richer chocolate flavor; for milder taste, substitute with chocolate powder.
  • To make larger bonbons, shape into larger spheres and chill longer before coating.

Storage

Store brigadeiros in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. For best texture, bring to room temperature for 10–15 minutes before serving.

Enjoy your homemade brigadeiros!
