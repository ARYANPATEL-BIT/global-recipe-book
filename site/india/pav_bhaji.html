Pav Bhaji

Ingredients:

  • Vegetables
    • Potatoes
    • Cauliflower
    • Carrots
    • Peas
    • Bell Peppers
  • Aromatics
    • Ginger-Garlic paste
    • Onions
    • Tomatoes
    • Green Chillies
  • Spices
    • Pav-Bhaji Masala
    • Turmeric Powder
    • Red Chilli Powder
    • Salt
  • Other:
    • Oil
    • Butter
    • Water
    • Pav
    • Lime Wedges
    • Coriander

Pav bhaji is a popular Indian street food from Mumbai consisting of a spiced vegetable mash (bhaji) served with soft buttered bread rolls (pav), typically garnished with butter, onions, and lemon.

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Vegetables: Boil potatoes, cauliflower, carrots, and peas in water until soft and mashable. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat Oil and Butter: In a large pan, heat oil and butter on medium heat.
  3. Sauté Aromatics: Add finely chopped onions and green chillies. Sauté until onions turn golden brown.
  4. Add Ginger-Garlic: Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Cook Tomatoes: Add finely chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  6. Add Spices: Add turmeric powder, red chilli powder, pav bhaji masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2 minutes.
  7. Add Bell Peppers: Add finely chopped bell peppers and sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  8. Mash Vegetables: Add the boiled vegetables to the pan and mash them thoroughly using a potato masher.
  9. Add Water: Add water to adjust consistency. The bhaji should be thick but not too dry. Simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  10. Toast Pav: On a separate griddle, apply butter on pav and toast them until golden brown on both sides.
  11. Final Touch: Add a generous dollop of butter on top of the bhaji and garnish with chopped coriander.
  12. Serve: Serve hot bhaji with toasted pav, chopped onions, and lime wedges on the side.

Pro-tip: Use generous amount of butter to make it creamy and delicious.
