A traditional South Indian tangy and spicy rice dish.

Total Time

40 min

Prep Time

15 min

Cook Time

25 min

Serves

3-4 people

Ingredients

For Rice

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably cooled)
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • Salt as needed

For Tamarind Paste

  • Small lemon-sized ball of tamarind (soaked in warm water)
  • 2 tbsp jaggery (optional, for mild sweetness)
  • 1¬Ω tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

For Tempering (Tadka)

  • 2 tbsp oil (sesame oil preferred)
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp chana dal (split Bengal gram)
  • 1 tsp urad dal (split black gram)
  • 2-3 dry red chilies
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 10-12 curry leaves
  • 2 tbsp peanuts
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)

Steps to Make Pulihora

  1. 1

    Cook and Prep the Rice

    • Cook 2 cups of rice.
    • Let the rice cool completely.
    • Spread it on a plate and drizzle 1 tbsp oil to keep the grains separate.
  2. 2

    Prepare Tamarind Extract

    • Soak tamarind in warm water for 10-15 minutes.
    • Squeeze the pulp and strain it to get thick tamarind juice.
    • Add turmeric, salt, and jaggery to the extract. Mix well and keep aside.
  3. 3

    Make the Tempering (Tadka)

    • Heat 2 tbsp oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add chana dal, urad dal, and peanuts. Fry until golden brown.
    • Add red chilies, green chilies, and curry leaves. Fry until aromatic.
    • Add a pinch of hing (asafoetida) and stir briefly.
  4. 4

    Cook the Tamarind Mix

    • Add the tamarind extract to the tempering pan.
    • Cook for about 5-7 minutes until the mixture thickens significantly and the oil starts to separate.
  5. 5

    Combine with Rice

    • Add the hot tamarind mixture to the cooled rice.
    • Mix gently until all the rice is evenly coated.
    • Adjust salt as needed.
  6. 6

    Rest and Serve

    • Let it sit for at least 30 minutes before serving for best flavor absorption.
    • Serve warm with papad or curd (yogurt).

Tips for the Best Pulihora

  • Use day-old or completely cooled rice for a better, non-sticky texture.
  • Sesame oil (Gingelly oil) is highly recommended as it greatly enhances the authentic South Indian flavor.
  • You can store the cooked tamarind paste in a jar for up to a week for quicker future preparations.
