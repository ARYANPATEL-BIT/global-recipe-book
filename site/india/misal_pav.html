Misal Pav

Spicy Maharashtrian street-food made with sprouted matki in fiery curry, topped with farsan & served with pav.

Maharashtra • Street Food
Spicy • Tangy
Serves 3–4

A fiery, flavorful misal curry served with buttered pav

Misal Pav is a beloved Maharashtrian breakfast and snack made from sprouted moth beans cooked in a spicy “kat” gravy, garnished with farsan, onions, coriander, and lemon. It’s hearty, spicy, and packed with textures.

Prep15 minutes
Cook30 minutes
DifficultyEasy – Medium
Vegan-Friendly Street Food Spicy Curry
Ingredients — For Misal
  • 1 cup sprouted moth beans (matki)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped
  • 1½ tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1½ tsp red chilli powder
  • 2 tsp goda masala (or garam masala)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water
For Kat (Spicy Curry)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 tbsp grated coconut
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • ½ tsp chilli powder
  • 1½ cups water
For Serving
  • Farsan or sev
  • Finely chopped onions
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • Lemon wedges
  • Pav (bread rolls)
Method
  1. Make Misal Masala: Dry-roast dried coconut, coriander seeds, red chillies, cloves, cinnamon, cardamoms, mace, star anise, peppercorns & stone flower. Add cumin, fennel, sesame & poppy seeds, then garlic. Cool and grind with dried ginger and chilli powder to a coarse masala.
  2. Sprout Matki: Soak moth beans 8 hours, wrap in moist cloth and keep warm to sprout (~5 hrs).
  3. Cook Usal (Sprouts Curry): Heat oil, add mustard & cumin seeds, curry leaves & hing. Add onions, then ginger-garlic-coconut mix & tomatoes. Add turmeric, chilli powder & misal masala. Add sprouts & water, boil, then add tamarind and optional jaggery. Simmer 7–8 min until sprouts soft and oil floats on top.
  4. Toast Pav: On griddle with butter or in oven (180 °C for 3–4 min) until golden.
  5. Assemble: Add usal to bowls, pour thin gravy (kat) on top, garnish with farsan, onion, coriander & lemon. Serve immediately with pav.
Serving Suggestion

Serve hot with buttered pav and a side of lemon & onions. Enjoy the contrast of spicy kat, crunchy farsan and soft bread.
