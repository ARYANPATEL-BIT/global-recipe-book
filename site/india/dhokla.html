Dhokla

Dhokla is a soft, fluffy, and savory steamed cake made from gram flour (besan), originating from the Indian state of Gujarat. It's a light and healthy snack that’s perfect for breakfast, tea-time, or even as a side dish. Dhokla is not fried, which makes it a healthy choice.

Prep Time: 10 min

Resting Time: 10–15 min

Cook Time: 15–20 min

Serving: 2 people

Ingredients

For Batter:
  • 1 cup Besan (gram flour)
  • 1 tbsp Lemon Juice
  • 1 tbsp Sugar
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 3/4 cup Water (adjust for a thick pouring consistency)
  • 1 tsp Eno Fruit Salt (or 1/2 tsp baking soda + 1/2 tsp citric acid)
For Tempering:
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • 1 tsp Mustard Seeds
  • 1–2 Green Chilies, slit or chopped
  • 8–10 Curry Leaves
  • 2 tbsp Water
  • 1 tsp Sugar (optional, for sweet tempering)
For Garnishing (optional):
  • Chopped coriander
  • Grated coconut

Instructions

  1. Make Batter: Mix besan, turmeric, salt, lemon juice, sugar, and water into a smooth, lump-free batter. Rest for 10–15 minutes.

  2. Prepare to Steam: Grease a steel plate or thali. Boil water in a steamer or large pot with a trivet.

  3. Add Eno & Steam: Add Eno, mix gently. Pour into the greased plate and steam for 15–20 mins. Check with a toothpick.

  4. Make Tempering: Heat oil, add mustard seeds, chilies, curry leaves, sugar, and water. Let it sizzle for a few seconds.

  5. Finish & Serve: Pour tempering over warm dhokla. Let it soak for a few minutes. Cut into pieces, garnish, and serve with chutney.
