TEMPURA

野菜天ぷら (Yasai Tempura)

A beloved Japanese dish featuring fresh vegetables coated in a light, crispy batter and deep-fried to golden perfection. The secret lies in its delicate, airy coating that creates a satisfying crunch with every bite.

Master Chef Tips

Keep the batter ICE COLD at all times. Add ice cubes to the batter and work quickly.
Do not overmix the batter—lumps are your friend! Overmixing develops gluten, making the coating heavy and chewy.
Maintain steady oil temperature throughout frying. If too low, tempura will absorb excess oil and become greasy.
Dry all vegetables thoroughly before battering—excess moisture will make the coating soggy.
Dust vegetables lightly with flour before dipping in batter for better adhesion.
Only fry a few pieces at a time to maintain oil temperature and ensure even cooking.
For extra crunch, drizzle a spoonful of batter over the tempura while it's frying in the oil.
Tempura is best enjoyed immediately. If storing, reheat in a 400°F (200°C) oven for 5 minutes to restore crispiness.
