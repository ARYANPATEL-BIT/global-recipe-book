Savory Ramen-Style Noodle Soup

Comforting bowl of noodles in a flavorful broth with tender meat and fresh vegetables.

Japanese-Inspired
Broth • Noodles
Serves 2

A simple, hearty ramen-style noodle soup

This everyday ramen combines an aromatic broth with tender slices of meat, sautéed vegetables, and springy noodles. It’s adaptable — use chicken, pork, or tofu and adjust seasoning for a lighter or richer bowl.

Prep15 minutes
Cook25 minutes
DifficultyEasy
Comfort Food Weeknight Customizable
Ingredients
  • Broth: 4 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp miso paste (optional)
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 1 tsp chili flakes (optional)
Soup & Garnish
  • 150 g noodles (ramen, soba, or egg noodles)
  • 150 g boneless chicken (thinly sliced) or tofu
  • ½ cup mushrooms, sliced
  • ½ cup bok choy or cabbage, chopped
  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 1 boiled egg, halved (optional)
  • Spring onions, chopped for garnish
Method
  1. Make the broth: In a large pot, heat sesame oil over medium heat. Sauté garlic and ginger until fragrant (30–40 seconds).
  2. Add liquids: Pour in the stock, soy sauce, and miso (if using). Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce to a simmer.
  3. Cook the protein & veg: Add chicken slices and simmer until cooked (6–8 minutes). Add mushrooms, bok choy, and carrots and cook for 3–4 minutes until tender-crisp.
  4. Cook noodles: Meanwhile, cook noodles separately according to package instructions. Drain and divide into serving bowls.
  5. Assemble: Place cooked noodles in bowls. Ladle hot broth, meat, and vegetables over the noodles. Top with halved egg and spring onions.
  6. Finish & serve: Add chili flakes or a dash of rice vinegar to taste. Serve immediately while hot.
Tips & Variations
  • Use pork belly or shredded rotisserie chicken for richer bowls.
  • Add kombu (seaweed) or a small piece of dried shiitake while simmering for extra umami.
  • Swap miso for a spoon of hoisin or oyster sauce for a different flavor profile.
